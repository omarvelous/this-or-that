# Brand Analysis

> Working from the product brief and technical architecture. The goal is to land
> on a name, voice, and visual direction that fits the product's personality and
> growth model.

---

## What the product actually is

A micro-polling app built around **head-to-head visual choices**. A maker drops
in two images (or text options), shares a link, and voters tap one — instantly,
no signup, no friction. Results animate live. The mechanic is always binary: even
with 3+ options (paid tier), it plays out as sequential A/B rounds
(king-of-the-hill).

**Core interaction loop:** See two things. Tap one. See what everyone else
picked.

---

## Brand attributes

These are the qualities the name, tone, and visuals should communicate:

| Attribute | Why it matters |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Instant** | One-tap voting, no accounts for voters, sub-second load |
| **Binary / dualistic** | The product is fundamentally about two things going head-to-head |
| **Playful** | Low-stakes, fun — not enterprise survey software |
| **Visual** | Image-first options, animated results, rich OG cards |
| **Social / viral** | Every shared link is acquisition; the product spreads through use |
| **Opinionated** | Encourages people to take a side, not sit on the fence |
| **Mobile-native feel** | Designed for thumb-tapping in a text thread or story |

### What the brand is NOT

- Corporate / enterprise (no "SurveyMonkey" energy)
- Complex / feature-heavy (no "build your form" positioning)
- Serious / high-stakes (no election-grade branding)
- Anonymous / generic (should have personality and edge)

---

## Audience

**Makers** (who create polls):
- Content creators polling their audience
- Friends settling debates ("which fit is better?")
- Small brands doing quick product validation
- Couples making decisions together (paint colors, dinner spots)
- Event planners, teachers, community managers

**Voters** (who tap and share):
- Anyone with a link — zero commitment required
- The kind of person who responds to Instagram polls and "which one?" stories

The brand needs to feel native to **group chats, stories, and text threads** —
not dashboards and admin panels.

## Competitive positioning

| Product | Positioning | Gap we fill |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Instagram Polls | Locked inside Instagram; ephemeral; no link sharing | Platform-agnostic, persistent, shareable |
| Strawpoll | Functional but bland; text-only; utilitarian | Visual-first, personality, mobile UX |
| Google Forms | General-purpose form builder | Purpose-built for binary visual choices |
| Twitter/X Polls | Text-only; locked inside platform | Image-first, works everywhere |

**Our wedge:** The only tool built specifically for **visual head-to-head
decisions** that lives on its own URL and works anywhere a link works.

## Name candidates

### Tier 1 — Strong contenders

These names are short, memorable, available-ish, and communicate the core
mechanic.

| Name | Rationale | Pros | Cons |
|------|-----------|------|------|
| **thisOrThat** | The current working name. Directly describes the interaction. Conversational, approachable. | Instantly understood; great for word-of-mouth ("make a thisOrThat"); natural hashtag | Long for a URL; common phrase so SEO/trademark could be noisy; no unique "brand word" |
| **Showdown** | Evokes competition and drama. A showdown between two options. | Energy and excitement; visual connotation; great for marketing ("Start a Showdown"); short | Could feel aggressive for casual use (paint colors); .com likely taken |
| **Callit** | "Call it" — like a coin toss or making a decision. Active verb. | Action-oriented; conversational ("just callit"); feels mobile-native; short | Could be confused with phone calling; less visual connotation |
| **Sided** | Taking sides. Implies the binary choice and the social/opinionated nature. | Clean and modern; works as noun ("make a Sided") and adjective; .co/.app likely available | Quieter energy; could feel political |
| **Tapoff** | One-tap + face-off. Combines the interaction (tap) with the mechanic (face-off). | Unique compound word; action-packed; great brand verb ("tap off"); mobile-native | Could be misread as "tap-off" (like a keg); less immediately obvious |

### Tier 2 — Worth discussing

| Name | Rationale | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Versus** | The purest head-to-head word. | Strong but possibly overused in gaming/esports; .com gone |
| **Flipside** | Two sides of a choice; playful. | Nice vibe but doesn't imply voting or decisions |
| **Matchup** | Head-to-head pairing — already used in the data model. | Sports-coded; might be too niche |
| **Hotake** | "Hot take" — opinionated, social, meme-native. | Very Gen Z; could age poorly; unclear pronunciation for some |
| **Pickr** | Short, action-oriented, modern vowel-drop naming. | Feels dated (Flickr era); too generic |
| **Tossup** | Close call, undecided — that's why you need to poll. | Fun and casual; slightly passive energy |

### Tier 3 — Creative swings

| Name | Vibe |
|------|------|
| **Duelo** | Spanish for "duel" — international flavor, unique sound |
| **Crowdcall** | Crowd wisdom + making a call — more descriptive than catchy |
| **SquareOff** | Competition framing — possibly too aggressive |

## Evaluation criteria

When picking the final name, weigh these:

1. **Say-it-out-loud test** — Can someone hear it once and spell it? Does it
   sound good in "make a ___" or "send them a ___"?
2. **Text-thread native** — Does it feel at home in an iMessage bubble? Or does
   it sound like enterprise software?
3. **Verb potential** — Can it become an action? ("Just ___ it" / "let's ___ this")
4. **Visual identity room** — Does the name suggest a logo, icon, or color
   direction? (Binary/split imagery, versus arrows, etc.)
5. **Domain/handle availability** — .com ideal, .co/.app acceptable. Check
   Twitter/Instagram handle availability.
6. **Trademark clearance** — Common words are harder to protect. Coined words
   are safer.
7. **SEO distinctiveness** — Searching the name should surface the product, not
   a million unrelated results.

## Initial voice and tone direction

Regardless of name, the brand voice should be:

- **Casual but confident** — talks like a friend, not a brand. "Which one's
  better? Make them pick." not "Create your poll today."
- **Short and punchy** — mirrors the one-tap interaction. No long copy.
- **Slightly provocative** — encourages opinions and debate. "Stop guessing.
  Ask everyone." / "Settle it."
- **Inclusive but opinionated** — everyone's invited, but you have to pick a side.

**Sample taglines** (name-agnostic):
- "Two options. One winner. You decide."
- "Settle it."
- "Make them pick."
- "The fastest way to ask everyone."

## Visual direction (preliminary)

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| **Logo mark** | Split/dualistic motif — a divided shape, two halves, "vs" symbology |
| **Color** | High contrast pair (not just one brand color — the brand IS about two things). Could shift per poll theme. |
| **Typography** | Bold, slightly condensed sans-serif for the wordmark. Confident, not delicate. |
| **Iconography** | Tap targets, comparison arrows, crown (for winners), split screens |
| **Motion** | Percentage bars animating, winner reveals, card flips — motion is part of the brand |
